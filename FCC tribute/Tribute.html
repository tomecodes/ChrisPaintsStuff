Alan Turing

Alan Turing was a British mathematician, logician, and cryptanalyst who is widely considered the father of computer science.

He played a pivotal role in breaking the German Enigma code during WWII.

Turing's Key Contributions

Turing Machine Binary logic Artificial Intelligence ACE (Automatic Computing Engine) Turing Test

Timeline

Here's a look at Alan Turing's life in key moments:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London
  • 1936 - Publishes foundational paper on computable numbers
  • 1939 - Begins codebreaking work at Bletchley Park
  • 1940 - Designs the Bombe machine
  • 1950 - Proposes the Turing Test
  • 1952 - Prosecuted for homosexuality
  • 1954 - Dies at age 41
  • 2009 - British government issues an apology
  • 2013 - Receives a royal pardon
  • 2021 - Appears on the £50 note

He continues to inspire generations of scientists, mathematicians, and thinkers.

Famous Quote

"We're sorry. You deserved so much better."

— British Prime Minister Gordon Brown, 2009

Extra Info

Turing’s contributions go beyond codebreaking. He laid the foundation for concepts we use today in computing and AI.

He also worked on mathematical biology, exploring how patterns like stripes on animals form naturally.

His life was cut tragically short, but his legacy continues to grow.
